Timeline

1817

The American School for the Deaf was established in Hartford, Connecticut.

1864

President Lincoln signed the charter bill that funded Gallaudet University, a university for students who are deaf or hard of hearing.

1935

President Roosevelt signed the Social Security Act, which provided unemployment insurance. It would later be amended to include a disability insurance program. Additional amendments to the Social Security Act would later establish Medicare and Medicaid, providing health care to individuals with disabilities.

1940

The National Federation of the Blind (NFB) was founded in Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania. The NFB continues to advocate for blind individuals today.

1963

President John F. Kennedy signed the Community Health Act, which shifted the delivery of mental health services from institutional settings to community settings.

1970

The Urban Mass Transportation Act was passed, which required buses and other public transportation to have wheelchair lifts.

1973

The Rehabilitation Act was passed. Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Law prohibits discrimination against people with disabilities in federal programs.

1977

Activists Judith Heumann, Kitty Cone, and Mary Jane Owen organized a sit-in to protest the then-U.S. Secretary of Health’s refusal to sign regulations for Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. The sit-in took place at the U.S. Department of Health office in San Francisco and lasted 28 days.

1978

In Denver, activists organized a sit-in and blocked the city’s buses with their wheelchairs. The purpose of this sit-in was to advocate for wheelchair-accessible transportation.

1990

President George H. W. Bush signed the American with Disabilities Act (ADA). The ADA required that governments provide accommodations for employees with disabilities. It also required public transportation to be accessible to individuals with disabilities.

1998

Section 508, an amendment made to the 1973 Rehabilitation Act, was signed into law. Section 508 requires federal government information technologies be accessible to people with disabilities.

2010

The Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act (ACA) was passed into law. The ACA prohibits insurance companies from dropping coverage due to illness or denying coverage because of pre-existing conditions.
